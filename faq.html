Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions


Q: What is the process for adopting a cat?

A: To adopt a cat, you need to fill out an adoption application form on our website. Once your application is reviewed and approved, we will schedule a home visit to ensure your home is suitable for the cat. If everything checks out, you can then proceed with the adoption process.

Q: How can I volunteer at the shelter?

A: We welcome volunteers! You can apply to volunteer by filling out the volunteer registration form available on our website under the "Registration" section.

Q: What should I do if I find a stray cat?

A: If you find a stray cat, please contact us immediately. We will provide guidance on the next steps, which may include bringing the cat to our shelter for care and potential rehoming.

Donation Questions


Q: How can I donate to the shelter?

A: Donations can be made through our website's donation page. We accept various forms of donations including monetary contributions, pet food, and other supplies.

Q: Can I set up a recurring donation?

A: Yes, you can set up a recurring donation on our donation page by selecting the option for monthly contributions.

Care and Health Questions


Q: What should I feed my cat?

A: We recommend a balanced diet of high-quality cat food, which includes both wet and dry food. Consult with your veterinarian for specific dietary needs for your cat.

Q: How often should I take my cat to the vet?

A: It's advisable to take your cat to the vet at least once a year for a check-up. Kittens and senior cats may require more frequent visits.
